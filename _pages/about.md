## Brief Biography

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Engineering at the University of Alabama
in Huntsville, specializing in quantum computing and distributed learning
systems. I am also a Graduate Research Assistant in the Network Intelligence
and Security Laboratory.

My research focuses on developing noise-resilient quantum machine learning
frameworks for noisy intermediate-scale quantum devices. In particular, I
work on quantum federated learning, quantum error mitigation, zero-noise
extrapolation, noise-aware training, personalized federated learning, and
distributed quantum systems.

My broader research goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical quantum
algorithms and practical quantum applications by improving the reliability,
scalability, and usability of quantum learning systems. I develop and
evaluate hybrid quantum-classical frameworks using quantum simulators,
high-performance computing environments, and real quantum hardware.

## Research

My current research interests include:

- Quantum Error Mitigation

- Noise-Aware Training
- Personalized Federated Learning

I have developed several open-source quantum computing frameworks, including
QuSenseSim, QuNoise, SimQFL, and ZNE-Rigetti. These projects support quantum
noise simulation, distributed quantum learning, quantum sensing, error
mitigation, visualization, and real-hardware experimentation.
