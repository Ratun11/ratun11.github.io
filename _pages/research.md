My research focuses on developing reliable, scalable, and noise-resilient
quantum learning systems for practical noisy intermediate-scale quantum
(NISQ) environments. I am particularly interested in the intersection of
quantum machine learning, distributed learning, quantum error mitigation,
and heterogeneous computing.

## Quantum Machine Learning

I develop hybrid quantum-classical learning frameworks based on variational
quantum circuits. My work studies how quantum noise, finite-shot measurement,
hardware limitations, and training instability affect the performance of
quantum learning models.

A major goal of this research is to improve the robustness and practical
usability of quantum machine learning on current quantum hardware.

## Quantum Federated Learning

My research investigates federated learning in distributed quantum
environments where participating clients may have different datasets,
quantum devices, noise conditions, computational capabilities, and
participation patterns.

I develop personalized and heterogeneity-aware quantum federated learning
methods that address:

- Non-IID data distributions
- Device and circuit heterogeneity
- Quantum noise and calibration drift
- Sporadic client participation
- Personalized quantum models
- Distributed quantum sensing

## Quantum Error Mitigation

I study methods for reducing errors in noisy quantum computations without
requiring fully fault-tolerant quantum hardware. My work includes:

- Zero-noise extrapolation
- Readout error mitigation
- Probabilistic error cancellation
- Dynamical decoupling
- Noise-aware quantum training
- Adaptive mitigation selection
- Cost-aware real-hardware execution

I have also evaluated zero-noise extrapolation on the Rigetti
Cepheus-1-108Q quantum processor through Amazon Braket.

## Quantum Sensing

I investigate distributed and collaborative quantum sensing systems under
heterogeneous noise conditions. This research uses metrics such as Fisher
Information and the Cramér–Rao Bound to evaluate sensing precision,
robustness, and collaboration among quantum sensors.

## Personalized and Distributed Learning

In addition to quantum learning, I work on personalized federated learning
for heterogeneous classical systems. My research considers uncertainty,
client-level differences, multimodal information, communication constraints,
and model personalization.

Application areas include:

- Smart-grid forecasting
- Wireless modulation recognition
- Air-quality estimation
- Anomaly detection
- Intelligent sensing
- Distributed edge systems
